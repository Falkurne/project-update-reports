MVP flow

The business logic should be explainable in one line: plate lookup, listing, bid, accept, contact.

1 Accounts set up

Dealer and broker roles are approved; Stripe supports both subscription types.

2 Plate lookup

CarJam fills vehicle details from number plate.

3 Dealer posts

Vehicle facts, buyer context, location, deadline.

4 Brokers bid

Rate, term, short conditions.

5 Dealer compares

Simple ranked list, no noisy auction board.

6 Dealer accepts

One broker wins. Listing closes.

7 Contacts unlock

Dealer and broker complete off-platform.

Decisions made

These are locked for the first MVP unless Freddie or Mike deliberately change direction.

Made

Dealer-led intro layer

Model A comes first: dealers post opportunities, brokers bid, dealers pick one broker.

Motofi is not the lender. Brokers complete the final finance work off-platform.

Made

CarJam lookup and fallback

Number-plate lookup is day-one scope because it makes listing creation easier.

If lookup fails or needs correction, allow dealer manual override and support-only override.

Made

CarJam stored fields

Store all CarJam vehicle fields except valuation.

Odometer should be manual entry only, not pulled as a trusted lookup value.

Made

Stripe subscriptions

Stripe is included from day one for both broker and dealer subscriptions.

Require Stripe payment before the paid action goes live.

Made

Buyer finance context

Vehicle-only listing data is not enough for the MVP.

Listings should include buyer income or credit band so brokers can make a useful bid.

Made

Semi-sealed bids

Dealers see submitted bids in a clean comparison list.

Brokers do not see competitor rates. No open rate board for the first MVP.

Made

Broker visible

Dealers see broker and company identity before accepting.

This gives the dealer enough trust context to choose without needing early contact unlock.

Made

Contact after accept

Contact details unlock only after the dealer accepts one broker.

No pre-accept reveal, unlock budget, or open contact marketplace in the MVP.

Made

Subscription structure

Create separate monthly subscription plans for brokers and dealers.

Exact dollar amounts are TBD, so Stripe price IDs should be configurable rather than hard-coded.

Made

Paid action gates

Require active Stripe payment before the action that creates value goes live.

Dealer posting and broker bidding/listing access should be gated by subscription status.

Made

Buyer field format

Use income band and credit band, with optional notes for extra context.

Avoid exact income capture and full finance application fields in the MVP.

Made

Bid validation

Use loose MVP validation with required rate, term, and conditions.

Tighten min/max rate and term rules later once pilot bid patterns are known.

Only TBD

The product direction is no longer blocked. Pricing amounts can be filled in once Freddie and Mike are ready.
