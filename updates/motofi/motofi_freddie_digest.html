Motofi MVP digest

Pick the marketplace before building the marketplace.

The current MVP is still dealer-led, but the latest chat makes the broker-buyer model a real strategic lane.

My call: build Model A first. Keep Model B visible in the strategy, pricing, and data model. Do not smuggle it into the MVP as extra fields and matching logic.

What changed

The new conversation confirms brokers sometimes bring buyers themselves. That makes Model B more than theoretical, but it also changes the product from deal bidding to buyer-to-inventory matching.

Build now

Model A

Dealers post finance needs. Brokers bid. Dealer accepts one. Fastest cold start and cleanest MVP.

Design for later

Model B

Brokers bring pre-qualified buyers. Dealers expose inventory/options. Stronger strategy, heavier product.

Not day one

Model C

Both flows run together. Best network effects, but too much chicken-and-egg for the first usable version.

Why Model A still wins MVP

Model A proves the exchange with less data, less compliance exposure, and fewer moving parts.

Cold start

One dealer can post one listing. Brokers can respond without the platform already having inventory depth.

Data burden

No buyer profile or credit pre-qualification is needed to test the core marketplace.

Business story

Conquest framing still works: dealers get finance support on deals they may not close anyway.

Scope control

Bid, accept, contact release, and audit logic are enough for a real pilot.

The listing-cap nuance

Fal was right to connect caps with dealer behaviour, and sheep was right that Model B wants dealers to post as many options as possible. Both are true in different models.

Model Listing policy Reason
Model A Cap live finance requests, or use a low pilot limit. Caps force award/cancel hygiene and reduce stale listings or contact farming.
Model B Allow more inventory/options, probably by account tier. Brokers with buyers need breadth. A hard cap fights the model.
Model C Use account tiers, quality scoring, and unlock budgets. Both sides need scale, but contact access and low-quality inventory need controls.
Do not treat listing limits as a product constant.

They are a business lever. For Model A, limits create urgency and clean outcomes. For Model B, limits should become packaging: Starter, Professional, Network Partner.

Account tiers are useful, but not all launch scope

The tier image gives a pricing story. It should not force AI pre-qual, APIs, dashboards, or white-label portals into the MVP.

Starter

Platform access, lender panel, compliance templates. Reasonable pilot packaging.

Professional

White-label portal, AI pre-qual, Akahu/Veille/PPSR APIs. Strong v2, heavy MVP.

Network Partner

Multi-seat licence, account manager, dashboards. Enterprise packaging after usage exists.

Build sequence

A digestible MVP path that preserves the option to move toward Model B without paying for it up front.

Model A pilotDealer posts finance deal, broker bids, dealer accepts, contacts unlock.
Policy layerAdd caps, unlock budgets, and account-tier limits as configurable rules.
Model B probeTest broker-brought buyer flow manually or with a thin intake form.
Combined networkOnly merge both sides once there is proof of repeat liquidity.

The next decision is not UI. It is whether Motofi starts as a dealer-posted deal board or a broker-driven buyer network.
